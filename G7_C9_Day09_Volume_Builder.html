Volume and Capacity

Three numbers, one quantity. Volume in cm³, capacity in mL, capacity in L — all the same amount, expressed in different units.

Volume (cm³)
V = l × w × h = 5 × 5 × 5

Capacity (mL)
1 cm³ = 1 mL → same number
125 mL
Capacity (L)
1 L = 1000 mL → divide by 1000
0.125 L
The 1 mL = 1 cm³ rule in action: the volume in cm³ and the capacity in mL are always the same number. This is the most useful conversion in all of metric. Toggle "Fill with water" to see what 1 mL × volume actually looks like inside the box.
