Closed cylinder surface area
SA = 2πr² + 2πrh
Why does the rectangle have width 2πr? When you unroll the curved side of the cylinder, the top edge (the rim of the top circle) becomes the top of the rectangle. The top edge of the cylinder IS the circumference of the base circle, which is 2πr. So the rectangle's width is 2πr. Its height is just h — the cylinder doesn't get taller or shorter when you unroll it, just flatter. That's why SA = 2πr² (the circles) + 2πrh (the rectangle).
