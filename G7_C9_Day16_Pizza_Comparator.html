Verdict
Option A (6 small) covers 12.56 m² more than Option B (2 big)!
The pizza insight (A = πr² in action): bigger pizzas almost always give more per dollar. A 14-inch pizza has 36% more area than a 12-inch. A 16-inch has 78% more than a 12-inch. This is because area scales with r² — doubling the radius gives 4× the area. Price usually grows slower than area, so bigger = better value.

The sprinkler insight: but more count can beat bigger radius! 6 sprinklers with r=3 cover more total area than 2 sprinklers with r=5 — because 6 × 9 = 54 beats 2 × 25 = 50 (in units of π). Count × r² is what matters, not r alone.
