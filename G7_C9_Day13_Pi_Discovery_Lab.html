Drag the Circle. Watch C/d.

Drag the small purple handle on the circle's edge to make the circle bigger or smaller. Watch the circumference C and diameter d change. The ratio C/d stays exactly the same — that's π.

Radius (r)
10cm
Diameter (d)
20cm
Circumference (C)
62.83cm
The Ratio C ÷ d
3.14159
This is π. Drag the circle to any size — the ratio doesn't change.
The discovery: no matter how big or small the circle is, the ratio C/d is exactly π ≈ 3.14159. The radius can be 3 cm or 60 cm or any number — the ratio stays the same. That's because π is a property of CIRCLES THEMSELVES, not of any particular circle. It is one of the most important constants in all of mathematics.
